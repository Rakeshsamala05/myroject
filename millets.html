Millets and ancient grains have long been the foundation of sustainable food systems, nourishing communities across the globe. Yet, in recent decades, these resilient and nutritious crops have been overshadowed by the rise in intensive mono-cropping. However, with the mounting challenges of climate change, food insecurity, and health crises, the millets and ancient grans are rediscovered for their incredible potential. This initiative, MAHARISHI, aims to revive and elevate millets and ancient grains, empowering farmers, improving nutrition, and fostering a more sustainable future for all.

Water-Efficient

Millets are highly water-efficient crops, requiring significantly less water than traditional cereals like wheat and rice. This makes them a crucial component of climate-smart agriculture in water-scarce regions.

Heat-Tolerant

Millets are adapted to thrive in high-temperature environments, making them more resilient to the impacts of climate change, such as rising temperatures and increased heat stress.

Nutrient-Efficient

Millets have a higher nutrient-use efficiency, allowing them to grow well in marginal soils with limited fertilizer inputs. This aligns with the principles of sustainable and regenerative agriculture.

Globally Adaptable

Millets and ancient grains are cultivated and consumed across a diverse range of regions, making them a truly global solution to food security and climate resilience.

Improved Nutrition

Millets and ancient grains are nutrient-dense, providing essential vitamins, minerals, and fiber to combat malnutrition and promote overall well-being.

Climate Resilience

These hardy crops can thrive in the face of climate change, helping to ensure food security and agricultural sustainability.

Empowered Farmers

MAHARISHI supports smallholder farmers by improving their access to resources, knowledge, and markets, empowering them to cultivate these crops successfully.

Revitalized Traditions

The initiative celebrates the rich cultural heritage and traditional knowledge associated with millets and ancient grains, ensuring their legacy is preserved for generations to come.

Teff, a tiny, nutrient-dense grain native to Ethiopia, is a gluten-free alternative to wheat with a unique, nutty flavor. It is a valuable source of protein, fiber, and essential minerals.

Quinoa, often referred to as the "superfood of the Andes," is a versatile ancient grain with a high protein content and a complete amino acid profile. It is also a good source of fiber, iron, and antioxidants.

Fonio, a small, fast-growing millet found in West Africa, is a highly nutritious grain that is rich in amino acids, minerals, and vitamins. It is also drought-resistant and well-suited to sustainable farming practices.

Amaranth, a nutritious, gluten-free pseudocereal, was a staple food of the Aztecs and is making a comeback in modern cuisine. It is packed with protein, fiber, and essential vitamins and minerals.

Job's tears (Coix lacryma-jobi) are nutrient-dense seeds that are high in protein, fiber, zinc, and calcium..

Connecting Researchers

MAHARISHI aims to establish mechanisms that connect researchers and institutions working on millets and ancient grains, facilitating the exchange of knowledge, resources, and research findings.

Building Platforms

The initiative will develop web-based platforms to disseminate information, share communication products, and encourage research and awareness among the global community.

Capacity Building

MAHARISHI will organize knowledge transfer, capacity-building activities, and international research workshops and conferences to support the development of a thriving global millet research community.

Incentivizing Innovation

The initiative will provide research and innovation prizes or awards to young scientists, encouraging them to pursue cutting-edge research on millets and ancient grains.
