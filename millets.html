The Versatility of Millets

Diverse Adaptability

Millets are a remarkably versatile group of small-seeded grasses that can thrive in a wide range of environments, from arid regions to tropical climates. These hardy crops are well-suited to cope with the challenges posed by climate change, including drought, heat, and poor soil conditions.

Nutritional Powerhouses

Millets are nutritional powerhouses, packed with essential vitamins, minerals, and fiber. They are particularly rich in protein, calcium, iron, and antioxidants, making them a valuable addition to any healthy diet. Millets also have a low glycemic index, making them a suitable option for those with diabetes or those seeking to manage their blood sugar levels.

Rediscovering the Power of Millets and Ancient Grains

Millets and ancient grains have long been the foundation of sustainable food systems, nourishing communities across the globe. Yet, in recent decades, these resilient and nutritious crops have been overshadowed by the rise in intensive mono-cropping. However, with the mounting challenges of climate change, food insecurity, and health crises, the millets and ancient grans are rediscovered for their incredible potential. This initiative, MAHARISHI, aims to revive and elevate millets and ancient grains, empowering farmers, improving nutrition, and fostering a more sustainable future for all.

MAHARISHI

Objectives

  1. 1 Establish mechanisms to connect researchers and institutions working on identified crops to enhance the dissemination of research findings and information, and identify research gaps and needs.
  2. 2 Establish web platforms to connect researchers, deliver information periodically and share communication products, and thematic briefs to encourage research and awareness.
  3. 3 Organizing knowledge transfer, on voluntary and mutually agreed terms, capacity-building activities, and international research workshops and conferences.
  4. 4 Provide research and innovation prizes or awards to young scientists to support their research interests. Also support open access publication of research results.
